Head Pain Management

Here you will find information about pain or other symptoms happening in the head or mind, and how to treat them.


Headache

A headache is pain happening in the head region. There are different types of headaches, depending on where the pain is, how it feels, and the pain itself. It could be sinus, menstrual, cluster, migraine, tension, and others.

To treat a headache, take aspirin, ibuprofen(Like Advil, Motrin IB, etc), and acetaminophen(Like Tylenol). There are alternatives, which include cognitive behavioral therapy, biofeedback. massage therapy, and acupuncture. For Migraines, rest in a quiet, dark, room, have hot or cold compresses to your head area, have a massage and an appropriate amount of caffeine, take over-the-counter medications, prescription medications, or preventive medocations.

Seek emergency if you have a severe headache, a headache after head injury of a fall, a fever and severe pain, and pain worsening certain treatment.



Fatigue

Fatique is a term to define an overall feeling of tiredness or a lack of motivation and energy. It can cause a variety of physical, mental, and emotional symptoms. The person having fatique is feeling overtired and has a strong desire to sleep that interferes with daily life activities.

There is no cure for chronic fatique syndrome. Treating depression can make it easier for people to cope with the problems associated with chronic fatique. Therapy is the most effective treatment for chronic fatique


Depression

Depression is a common but critical mood disorder. It causes severe symptomss that affect how the affected person feels, thinks, and handles daily life, such as sleeping, eating, and working.

Depression is usually treated with medication and/or psychotherapy. Antidepressants are medications can help improve how the brain uses certain chemicals that control emotions. They take usually two or four weeks to work. Psychotherapies, or 'talk therapies' can also help people with depression. Types are cognitive behavioral therapy, interpersonal therapy, and problem solving therapy.



Insomnia

Insomnia is a sleeping disorder with difficulty falling asleep. People with this wake up often during the night and have trouble going back to sleep. Most of this is related to poor sleeping habits, depression, anxiety, or certain medications.

Acute insomnia may not need treatment, but mild insomnia can be prevented with good sleep habits. Short acting drugs can help avoid effects such as drowsiness. Chronic insomnia includes first treating underlying conditions or health problems. Relaxation exercises, sleep restriction therapy, and reconditioning can also help.
